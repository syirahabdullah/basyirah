BSF Larvae Biomass Growth & Derivative Optimization

Utilizing calculus to identify peak feed conversion points for Black Soldier Fly Larvae (*Hermetia illucens*) reared on local commercial food waste in Malaysia.

Reference Dataset MARDI Agro-waste Circular Bioeconomy

The Food Security Challenge

Malaysia is highly vulnerable to animal feed ingredient price spikes. Rearing high-protein larvae on agricultural waste offers a domestic alternative.

To optimize operations, we must harvest larvae exactly at their **maximum rate of daily biomass gain** (the derivative's absolute maximum point). Harvesting too early sacrifices weight; harvesting too late yields low-protein, high-chitin prepupae, which are nutritionally poor for livestock.

The Mathematical Model

Cumulative Biomass Function:
$$f(x) = \frac{L}{1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)}}$$
Modeling BSF growth limits. Where $L = 200\text{ mg}$ max larval weight, $k = 0.45\text{ growth rate coefficient}$, $x_0 = 10\text{ days (inflection point)}$.
Instantaneous Growth Rate (First Derivative):
$$f'(x) = \frac{L \cdot k \cdot e^{-k(x - x_0)}}{(1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)})^2}$$
Calculated using the calculus chain rule. Measures instantaneous weight gain in $\text{mg/day}$.
